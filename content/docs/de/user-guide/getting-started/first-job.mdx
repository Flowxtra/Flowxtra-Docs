# Creating Your First Job

This guide will walk you through creating and publishing your first job posting on Flowxtra. By the end, you'll have a professional job listing that's optimized for search engines and ready to attract qualified candidates.

<Callout type="info">
**Time Required**: About 10-15 minutes to create a complete job posting.
</Callout>

## Before You Start

Make sure you have:

- Completed your company profile
- Determined the job title and requirements
- Prepared a job description (or use our AI generator)
- Decided on salary range and benefits

## Creating Your Job Posting

<Steps>
<Step>
### Access the Job Creation Form

1. Log in to your Flowxtra dashboard
2. Click the **Create Job** button in the top right corner
3. You'll be taken to the job creation form

Alternatively, navigate to **Jobs** > **Create New Job** from the sidebar.
</Step>

<Step>
### Enter Basic Information

Fill in the essential job details:

#### Job Title

Enter a clear, descriptive job title that candidates will search for.

**Good Examples**:
- Senior Software Engineer
- Marketing Manager
- Customer Support Specialist

**Avoid**:
- Rockstar Developer (overly casual)
- Ninja Coder (unclear)
- Person Needed (too vague)

<Callout type="info">
Use standard industry job titles for better search visibility.
</Callout>

#### Department

Select or create a department for this position:
- Engineering
- Marketing
- Sales
- Customer Support
- Human Resources
- Operations
- Finance

#### Employment Type

Choose the type of employment:
- Full-time
- Part-time
- Contract
- Temporary
- Internship
- Freelance

#### Work Location

Specify where the work will be performed:
- **On-site**: Select your office location
- **Remote**: Specify if worldwide or specific regions
- **Hybrid**: Indicate required office days
</Step>

<Step>
### Write the Job Description

Create a compelling job description that includes:

#### Company Overview

Brief introduction to your company (2-3 sentences):

```
At TechCorp, we're building the future of cloud computing. 
Our team of 50+ engineers serves over 10,000 customers worldwide, 
and we're growing fast.
```

#### Role Summary

What the person will do (3-4 sentences):

```
We're looking for a Senior Software Engineer to join our Platform team. 
You'll design and build scalable backend services, mentor junior engineers, 
and help shape our technical direction. This is a high-impact role with 
significant autonomy and growth potential.
```

#### Responsibilities

List key responsibilities (5-8 bullet points):

- Design and implement scalable backend services
- Write clean, maintainable, well-tested code
- Participate in code reviews and technical discussions
- Mentor junior team members
- Collaborate with product and design teams
- Contribute to technical architecture decisions

#### Requirements

List required qualifications (5-7 bullet points):

- 5+ years of software engineering experience
- Strong proficiency in Python or Go
- Experience with distributed systems and microservices
- Solid understanding of databases (SQL and NoSQL)
- Excellent communication and collaboration skills
- Bachelor's degree in Computer Science or equivalent experience

#### Nice to Have

Optional qualifications (3-5 bullet points):

- Experience with Kubernetes and Docker
- Contributions to open-source projects
- Experience in a startup environment
- Knowledge of machine learning concepts

<Callout type="info">
**Pro Tip**: Use our AI-powered job description generator to create professional descriptions in seconds. Click **Generate with AI** and provide key details.
</Callout>
</Step>

<Step>
### Set Salary and Benefits

#### Salary Information

Choose how to display salary:

**Option 1: Salary Range**
- Minimum: $100,000
- Maximum: $140,000
- Currency: USD
- Period: Per year

**Option 2: Fixed Salary**
- Amount: $120,000
- Currency: USD
- Period: Per year

**Option 3: Don't Specify**
- Hide salary information

<Callout type="warn">
Jobs with visible salary information receive 30% more applications on average.
</Callout>

#### Benefits

List benefits and perks:

- Health insurance (medical, dental, vision)
- 401(k) with company match
- Unlimited PTO
- Remote work flexibility
- Professional development budget
- Home office stipend
- Equity/stock options
</Step>

<Step>
### Configure Application Settings

#### Application Method

Choose how candidates will apply:

**Flowxtra Application Form** (Recommended)
- Candidates apply directly through Flowxtra
- All applications in one dashboard
- Automated email notifications

**External URL**
- Redirect to your ATS or career page
- Track applications externally

#### Required Information

Select what information to collect:

**Standard Fields**:
- Full name (required)
- Email address (required)
- Phone number (optional)
- Resume/CV (required)
- Cover letter (optional)
- LinkedIn profile (optional)

#### Screening Questions

Add custom questions to filter candidates:

**Example Questions**:

1. "Are you authorized to work in the United States?"
   - Type: Yes/No
   - Required: Yes

2. "How many years of Python experience do you have?"
   - Type: Multiple Choice
   - Options: 0-2, 2-5, 5-10, 10+
   - Required: Yes

3. "Why are you interested in this position?"
   - Type: Long Text
   - Required: No

<Callout type="info">
Limit screening questions to 3-5 to avoid deterring candidates.
</Callout>
</Step>

<Step>
### Review and Publish

#### Preview Your Job

Click **Preview** to see how your job will appear to candidates:

- Check for typos and formatting
- Verify all information is accurate
- Ensure links work correctly
- Review on mobile view

#### SEO Optimization

Flowxtra automatically optimizes your job for search engines:

- Generates structured data for Google for Jobs
- Creates SEO-friendly URL
- Adds relevant meta tags
- Optimizes for mobile

#### Publishing Options

Choose when to publish:

**Publish Now**
- Job goes live immediately
- Starts accepting applications right away

**Schedule for Later**
- Set a future publish date and time
- Useful for coordinated launches

**Save as Draft**
- Save your work without publishing
- Come back to finish later

Click **Publish Job** to make your job live!
</Step>
</Steps>

## After Publishing

### Share Your Job

Once published, share your job to attract candidates:

1. **Copy the job URL** from your dashboard
2. **Share on social media**:
   - LinkedIn
   - Twitter
   - Facebook
3. **Post to job boards**:
   - Indeed
   - Glassdoor
   - LinkedIn Jobs
4. **Email your network**
5. **Add to your website** using the job widget

### Monitor Performance

Track your job's performance in the dashboard:

- **Views**: How many people viewed your job
- **Applications**: Number of applications received
- **Conversion Rate**: Views to applications ratio
- **Traffic Sources**: Where candidates found your job

### Manage Applications

As applications come in:

1. Review new applications daily
2. Move qualified candidates through your pipeline
3. Respond to candidates promptly
4. Schedule interviews with top candidates

## Tips for Success

### Write Clear Job Titles

Use standard, searchable job titles instead of creative ones:

- Use: "Software Engineer" not "Code Wizard"
- Use: "Marketing Manager" not "Growth Hacker"
- Use: "Sales Representative" not "Revenue Ninja"

### Be Specific About Requirements

Clearly distinguish between required and preferred qualifications:

- **Required**: Must-have skills and experience
- **Nice to Have**: Bonus qualifications

### Include Salary Information

Jobs with salary information get more applications:

- Specify a range rather than exact amount
- Be realistic and competitive
- Consider total compensation, not just base salary

### Optimize for Mobile

Over 60% of job seekers use mobile devices:

- Keep descriptions concise
- Use bullet points for readability
- Avoid large blocks of text
- Test on mobile before publishing

### Use Keywords

Include relevant keywords for better search visibility:

- Job title variations
- Required skills and technologies
- Industry-specific terms
- Location information

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Too Many Requirements

Don't create an impossible wish list:

- Focus on essential qualifications
- Separate required from preferred
- Consider training potential

### Vague Job Descriptions

Be specific about the role:

- Describe actual day-to-day work
- Explain team structure
- Clarify reporting relationships

### Missing Salary Information

Candidates want to know compensation:

- At minimum, provide a range
- Include benefits and perks
- Mention equity if applicable

### Complicated Application Process

Keep it simple:

- Limit screening questions
- Don't require unnecessary information
- Make mobile application easy

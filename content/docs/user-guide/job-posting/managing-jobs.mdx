# Managing Jobs & Candidates

The **Jobs Dashboard** is your central hub for tracking and managing all your recruitment efforts. This guide covers everything from managing job statuses to reviewing candidates and optimizing your hiring process.

## The Jobs Dashboard

Navigate to **Jobs** in the main menu to see a comprehensive list of all your recruitment activities.

### Dashboard Overview

The Jobs Dashboard provides a complete view of your hiring pipeline with real-time metrics and advanced filtering options.

**Main Columns**:
- **Job Title**: Display name with job ID reference
- **Status**: Visual indicator (Live, Closed, Draft, Paused, Expired)
- **Views**: Total page views and unique visitors
- **Applications**: Number of received applications
- **Candidates**: Total candidates in pipeline (including manually added)
- **Posted Date**: Publication date with time elapsed
- **Location**: Work location (on-site/remote)
- **Actions**: Quick action buttons (Edit, Share, Preview, Close)

### Status Indicators

Jobs can be in one of the following statuses:

**Live** (Green badge):
- Job is publicly visible
- Accepting applications
- Indexed by search engines
- Distributed to job boards

**Draft** (Gray badge):
- Job is saved but not published
- Only visible to team members
- Can be edited freely
- No expiration

**Closed** (Red badge):
- No longer accepting applications
- Still visible to public (shows "Position Filled")
- Candidate data preserved
- Can be reopened

**Paused** (Yellow badge):
- Temporarily hidden from public view
- Applications paused
- Can be resumed anytime
- Useful for holding/reviewing

**Expired** (Orange badge):
- Automatically closed after 45 days
- No longer visible to candidates
- Can be renewed to republish

<Callout type="info">
Jobs automatically expire 45 days after publishing to keep your job board fresh and relevant. You'll receive an email notification 7 days before expiration.
</Callout>

### Filtering and Search

**Search Bar**:
- Search by job title, ID, or keywords
- Real-time search results
- Fuzzy matching for typos

**Status Filters (Tabs)**:
- **All**: View all jobs regardless of status
- **Active**: Currently live jobs only
- **Drafts**: Unpublished jobs
- **Closed**: Filled or inactive positions
- **Expired**: Jobs past 45-day limit

**Advanced Filters**:
- **Date Range**: Filter by posting date
- **Location**: Filter by office/city/remote
- **Department**: Filter by department or team
- **Applications**: Show only jobs with/without candidates
- **Performance**: Sort by views, application rate

**Sort Options**:
- Newest first (default)
- Oldest first
- Most applications
- Most views
- Expiring soon
- Alphabetical (A-Z)

### Bulk Actions

Select multiple jobs using checkboxes to perform batch operations:

**Available Bulk Actions**:
- Close multiple jobs at once
- Pause/Resume multiple jobs
- Export selected jobs to CSV/Excel
- Share multiple jobs on social media
- Delete multiple draft jobs
- Change status for selected jobs

<Callout type="tip">
Use bulk actions to efficiently manage seasonal hiring campaigns or end-of-quarter cleanup.
</Callout>

### Data Export

Export your job data for reporting and analysis:

**Export Options**:
1. Click **Export** button in top right
2. Select export format:
   - **CSV**: For Excel/Google Sheets
   - **Excel**: Pre-formatted spreadsheet
   - **PDF**: Printable report
3. Choose data to include:
   - Job details (title, description, location)
   - Application statistics (views, applications, conversion rate)
   - Candidate data (names, status, ratings)
   - Performance metrics (time-to-hire, source tracking)
4. Apply filters before export (optional)
5. Download file

**What Gets Exported**:
- All visible job fields
- Application counts and status breakdown
- Candidate information (respects privacy settings)
- Analytics and performance data
- Team comments and ratings (if selected)

<Callout type="warn">
Exported files may contain sensitive candidate data. Handle according to your company's data protection policies and GDPR requirements.
</Callout>

## Job Details View

When you click on a specific job, you enter the **Job Details View** - your command center for managing that position.

### Overview Section

**Quick Stats Card** displays:
- **Total Applicants**: Number of candidates who applied
- **Pipeline**: Breakdown by stage (New, Screening, Interview, Offer, Hired, Rejected)
- **Views**: Page views and unique visitors
- **Conversion Rate**: Applications per 100 views
- **Time to Hire**: Average days from posting to hire
- **Application Source**: Where candidates found the job

### Action Buttons

Located at the top of the job details page:

**Preview** (Eye icon):
- See exactly what candidates see
- Opens in new tab
- Test application form
- Check mobile responsiveness
- Verify all links and content

<Callout type="tip">
Always preview your job before sharing to ensure it appears correctly across devices.
</Callout>

**Share** (Share icon):
Opens sharing menu with multiple options:

**Direct Link**:
- Copy unique application URL
- Paste in emails, messages, or ads
- Track link clicks (if UTM parameters added)

**Social Media**:
- Share to LinkedIn, Facebook, Twitter/X
- Pre-filled post with job highlights
- Auto-includes company branding
- Schedule for optimal posting times

**QR Code**:
- Generate scannable QR code
- Download as PNG or SVG
- Print on flyers, posters, business cards
- Track scans and applications

**Email**:
- Send to specific email addresses
- Use pre-designed email template
- Personalize message
- Track opens and clicks

**Embed Widget**:
- Get embed code for your website
- Customize appearance (colors, layout)
- Responsive design
- Updates automatically when job details change

<Callout type="info">
Social media posts shared directly from Flowxtra include rich previews with your company logo and job details for better engagement.
</Callout>

**Edit** (Pencil icon):
- Modify job details
- Update requirements or benefits
- Change salary range
- Edit description

**More Options** (Three dots):
- Close job
- Pause job
- Duplicate job
- Export job data

- View analytics

### Add Candidate Manually

Sometimes you receive resumes outside the platform (email, LinkedIn, referrals, career fairs). The **Add Candidate** feature lets you import them into your pipeline.

**How to Add a Candidate**:

1. Click **+ Add Candidate** button in top right
2. Fill in candidate information:
   - **Personal Details**:
     - Full Name (required)
     - Email Address (required)
     - Phone Number (optional)
     - LinkedIn Profile URL (optional)

   - **Application Details**:
     - Upload Resume (PDF, DOC, DOCX - max 10MB)
     - Cover Letter (optional)
     - Application Source (e.g., "LinkedIn Outreach", "Email", "Referral")
     - Referrer Name (if applicable)

   - **Initial Assessment** (optional):
     - Add notes about the candidate
     - Assign initial rating (1-5 stars)
     - Tag skills manually
     - Set initial pipeline stage

3. Click **Add to Pipeline**

**What Happens Next**:
- Candidate appears in your pipeline immediately
- Marked as "Manually Added" for tracking
- Resume is parsed automatically (if uploaded)
- Can be moved through pipeline like any other candidate
- Receives automated emails based on stage (optional)

<Callout type="tip">
When adding candidates manually, include detailed notes about how you found them and why they're a good fit. This helps team members understand context.
</Callout>

**Bulk Import**:
If you have multiple candidates to add:
1. Click **Bulk Import** instead
2. Download CSV template
3. Fill in candidate details (Name, Email, Resume URL, etc.)
4. Upload completed CSV
5. Review and confirm import
6. All candidates added to pipeline at once

**Privacy Note**:
Manually added candidates must still consent to data processing. Ensure you have permission to store their information in your ATS.

## Editing a Job

You can edit a job at any time, even if it's live.

1. Find the job in the dashboard
2. Click the **Edit** (pencil) icon
3. Make your changes
4. Click **Update Job**

<Callout type="warn">
**Caution**: Changing key details like the "Job Title" or "Location" of a live job may reset its SEO ranking on Google. For minor typos, it's fine. For major changes, consider closing the job and creating a new one.
</Callout>

## Closing a Job

When you have found your hire or want to stop receiving applications:

1. Click the **More Options** (three dots) menu
2. Select **Close Job**
3. Confirm the action

**Effect**:
- The public job page will show "Position Filled" or "No longer accepting applications"
- Candidates can no longer apply
- The job moves to the "Closed" tab
- All candidate data is preserved

## Reopening a Job

If a hire falls through or you need another person for the same role:

1. Go to the **Closed** tab
2. Find the job
3. Select **Revert to Draft** or **Republish**
4. Review details and publish again

**When to Reopen**:
- Candidate declined offer
- Need to hire multiple people for same role
- Previous hire didn't work out
- Budget approved for another position

## Deleting a Job

**Deletion is permanent.** It removes the job posting and disassociates it from applications (though candidate data may be preserved depending on settings).

1. Click **More Options**
2. Select **Delete**
3. Type "DELETE" to confirm

<Callout type="tip">
We recommend **Closing** jobs instead of **Deleting** them so you preserve the historical data and analytics.
</Callout>

**What Gets Deleted**:
- Job posting and all details
- Job URL becomes inactive
- Job removed from all dashboards
- Analytics data may be archived

**What's Preserved**:
- Candidate applications and resumes
- Team comments and ratings
- Historical data (if enabled in settings)

## Managing Candidates

Once applications start coming in, you'll use the **Candidates** tab within each job to review and manage applicants.

### Candidate View Options

Switch between different views to match your workflow:

**List View** (Default):
- Compact rows showing key info
- Quick scan of all candidates
- Sortable columns
- Bulk selection checkboxes

**Kanban View** (Pipeline):
- Visual drag-and-drop board
- Columns represent pipeline stages
- Move candidates by dragging cards
- See counts per stage

**Table View** (Detailed):
- Spreadsheet-like layout
- More columns visible
- Advanced sorting and filtering
- Export-friendly

**Grid View** (Cards):
- Profile card layout
- Shows candidate photo (if available)
- Star ratings visible
- Great for team reviews

### Candidate Information

Each candidate profile shows:

**Header**:
- Name and profile photo
- Current pipeline stage
- Star rating (1-5)
- Application date
- Source (e.g., "Indeed", "Company Website", "LinkedIn")

**Contact Info**:
- Email address (click to send)
- Phone number (click to call)
- LinkedIn profile link
- Location/City

**Application Materials**:
- Resume (view/download PDF)
- Cover letter (if provided)
- Portfolio link (if provided)
- Additional documents

**AI-Powered Insights** (if enabled):
- Skills match percentage
- Experience match score
- Education match
- Key highlights from resume
- Red flags or concerns
- Suggested interview questions

**Application Responses**:
- Answers to custom questions
- Video introduction (if requested)
- Assessment results
- Test scores

**Activity Timeline**:
- Application submitted
- Stage changes
- Emails sent/received
- Interview scheduled
- Notes added
- Files uploaded

### Filtering Candidates

**Quick Filters**:
- **New**: Applications from last 7 days
- **Unreviewed**: Not yet opened
- **Favorites**: Starred candidates
- **Scheduled**: Interviews booked
- **Hired**: Successfully hired

**Advanced Filters**:
- **Skills**: Filter by specific skills
- **Experience**: Years of experience range
- **Education**: Degree level or institution
- **Location**: City, country, or remote preference
- **Salary Expectations**: Within specific range
- **Source**: Application channel
- **Rating**: Star rating (1-5 stars)
- **Stage**: Pipeline stage
- **Tags**: Custom tags applied

**Custom Saved Filters**:
- Save frequently used filter combinations
- Quick access from dropdown
- Share with team members
- Update or delete anytime

### Taking Action on Candidates

**Single Candidate Actions**:

**Move to Stage**:
- Select new pipeline stage from dropdown
- Optionally send automated email notification
- Add note about reason for move
- Update candidate status

**Send Email**:
- Choose from email templates (rejection, interview invitation, etc.)
- Personalize with candidate name and details
- Attach documents (offer letter, onboarding info)
- Schedule send for later
- Track opens and clicks

**Schedule Interview**:
- Choose interview type (phone, video, in-person)
- Select available time slots
- Assign interviewers from team
- Send calendar invitations
- Add video meeting link (Zoom, Teams, Google Meet)
- Automated reminders sent 24hrs and 1hr before

**Add to Favorites**:
- Star candidates for quick access
- Create shortlist for hiring manager review
- Useful for passive candidates to revisit later

**Disqualify/Reject**:
- Select rejection reason
- Send rejection email (optional)
- Add internal notes
- Candidate moved to "Rejected" stage
- Can be reconsidered later if needed

**Export Profile**:
- Download candidate profile as PDF
- Include resume, application answers, and notes
- Share with stakeholders outside the system

**Bulk Candidate Actions**:

Select multiple candidates using checkboxes:

- **Move Selected**: Change stage for all at once
- **Send Bulk Email**: Same message to multiple candidates
- **Bulk Reject**: Reject multiple applicants
- **Export Selected**: Download multiple profiles
- **Add Tags**: Tag group of candidates
- **Assign Reviewer**: Assign team member to review

<Callout type="warn">
When sending bulk emails, personalize where possible. Generic mass emails have lower engagement and may harm your employer brand.
</Callout>

### Collaboration Features

**Team Comments**:
- Add internal notes visible only to team
- @mention team members to notify
- Reply to comments for discussion
- Mark comments as resolved

**Candidate Ratings**:
- Rate candidates 1-5 stars
- See team average rating
- Add rating categories (Technical Skills, Culture Fit, Communication)
- View individual team member ratings

**Assign Reviewers**:
- Assign specific team members to review candidates
- Set review deadlines

   - Contact information

## Best Practices for Job Management

### Keeping Jobs Fresh

**Regular Updates**:
- Review job descriptions monthly
- Update salary ranges to match market
- Refresh benefits list with new perks
- Remove outdated requirements

**Monitor Performance**:
- Check weekly application rates
- Compare to industry benchmarks
- Adjust if getting too few or too many applications
- Optimize job titles for searchability

**Renew Before Expiration**:
- Renew jobs 40-45 days after posting
- Update posting date for better visibility
- Maintains SEO rankings
- Shows candidates it's an active search

### Managing Application Volume

**High Volume (100+ applications)**:
- Use knockout questions to pre-screen
- Enable AI-powered resume screening
- Set up automated rejection emails
- Assign multiple team members to review

**Low Volume (fewer than 10 applications)**:
- Review job title for clarity
- Expand remote/location options
- Increase salary range visibility
- Promote on more channels
- Extend application deadline

### Team Collaboration

**Assign Clear Roles**:
- Recruiter: Initial screening
- Hiring Manager: Final decision
- Team Leads: Technical assessment
- HR: Compliance and offers

**Set Response Time Goals**:
- Acknowledge applications within 24hrs
- First screening within 3 days
- Interview scheduling within 1 week
- Final decision within 2 weeks post-interview

**Regular Sync Meetings**:
- Weekly pipeline review
- Discuss top candidates
- Address bottlenecks
- Align on hiring criteria

### Compliance and Privacy

**GDPR Compliance**:
- Obtain consent before storing candidate data
- Allow candidates to request data deletion
- Don't store data longer than necessary
- Document legal basis for processing

**Fair Hiring Practices**:
- Use objective evaluation criteria
- Avoid discriminatory questions
- Review for unconscious bias
- Document hiring decisions

**Data Security**:
- Limit access to authorized team members only
- Use strong passwords and 2FA
- Don't share candidate data externally
- Regularly audit access logs

<Callout type="tip">
Create a hiring checklist for each role to ensure consistent, compliant evaluation of all candidates.
</Callout>

### Performance Tracking

**Key Metrics to Monitor**:
- **Time to Fill**: Days from posting to hire
- **Application Rate**: Applications per 100 views
- **Quality of Hire**: Performance of hired candidates after 90 days
- **Source Effectiveness**: Which channels bring best candidates
- **Interview-to-Offer Ratio**: Efficiency of interview process
- **Offer Acceptance Rate**: Attractiveness of offers

**Use Analytics to Improve**:
- Identify drop-off points in funnel
- Optimize job descriptions based on what works
- Allocate budget to best-performing channels
- Refine screening criteria over time

### Candidate Experience

**Communication Best Practices**:
- Set expectations upfront (timeline, process)
- Provide regular updates even if no decision yet
- Send personalized rejections (avoid generic templates)
- Ask for feedback from candidates
- Respond to questions within 1 business day

**Make Application Easy**:
- Mobile-friendly application forms
- Keep forms short (under 10 minutes)
- Allow "Apply with LinkedIn"
- Don't ask for redundant information
- Provide clear instructions

**Positive Rejection Experience**:
- Be respectful and appreciative
- Provide constructive feedback (optional but valued)
- Invite to apply for future roles
- Keep door open for other opportunities
- Thank them for their time

<Callout type="tip">
Every candidate interaction affects your employer brand. Even rejected candidates should leave with a positive impression of your company.
</Callout>

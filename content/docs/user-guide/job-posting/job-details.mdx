# Job Details

The **Job Details** section is the core of your posting. It tells candidates what the role involves and what you're looking for.

## Job Title

Enter a clear, standard job title.

- **Best Practice**: Use industry-standard terms (e.g., "Sales Manager" vs. "Sales Ninja")
- **Keywords**: Include level (Senior, Junior) and specialization (Frontend, B2B)
- **Length**: Keep it concise (10-80 characters)

## Job Description

This is your chance to sell the role and your company.

### Structure

A good job description includes:

1. **Role Summary**: 2-3 sentences about the main purpose of the job.
2. **Company Culture**: Why is your company a great place to work?
3. **Key Responsibilities**: What will the person do every day?
4. **Who You Are**: The ideal candidate profile.

### Rich Text Editor

Use the editor to format your description:

- **Bold** for emphasis
- **Bullet points** for readability
- **Headers** to organize sections
- **Links** to your website or portfolio

<Callout type="tip">
**Use AI Assist**: Click the "Magic Wand" icon to have Flowxtra's AI improve your writing or generate a description from scratch based on the job title.
</Callout>

## Requirements & Qualifications

Clearly list what is needed for the role.

### Required Skills
List the non-negotiable skills.
- Education level
- Years of experience
- Specific software or tools (e.g., "React", "Salesforce")
- Certifications

### Preferred Qualifications
List "nice-to-have" skills that would make a candidate stand out but aren't strictly necessary.

## Employment Details

### Employment Type
Select one or more:
- Full-time
- Part-time
- Contractor
- Temporary
- Internship

### Experience Level
- Entry Level
- Associate
- Mid-Senior Level
- Director
- Executive

### Location
- **On-site**: Requires physical presence
- **Remote**: Work from home
- **Hybrid**: Mix of both

<Callout type="info">
For **Remote** jobs, you can specify if it's "Worldwide" or restricted to specific regions/timezones.
</Callout>

## Categories & Tags

Help candidates find your job by categorizing it correctly.

- **Category**: Select the primary function (e.g., "Design", "Engineering")
- **Tags**: Add specific keywords (e.g., "UI/UX", "JavaScript", "Startup")
